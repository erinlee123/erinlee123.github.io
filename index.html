El Pit Cenote - Tulum, Mexico 7/9/2021

The cenote known as "The Pit" is easily my favorie dive site. The Pit is the deepest cenote on the Yucatan Peninsula, with a maximum depth of 390ft and it's apart of the longest underwater cave system in the world, Sac Aktun. Located 45 km south of Playa del Carmen and 15 km north of Tulum, inside the Dos Ojos National Park. From the surface the cenote looks like a mere sinkhole in the middle of the jungle, small in size. Your dive equipment wiill be lowered down with a pulley, allowing you to gear up in the water. The cenote appears unimpressive at first glance, but the instant you submerge the scene completely transforms into massive underwater cavern. Light shines through the small surface opening creating a great, beautiful beam that illuminates the cavern creating a spectacular visual. As you descend through the freshwater you will go through a halocline, which will create a visual similar to the blurring effect that radiates off hot pavent, and then you enter the salt water. You will descend slowly, moving along the cave walls gazing at the enormous stalagmites lining the entire cave. Once you reach 30 meters you will move through an immense hydrogen sulfide cloud, swimming over it gives the illusion that you are flying. From the cloud, branches of a large petrified tree protrude adding an eery effect. Swimming through the cloud, passing through the petrified tree branches is absolutely mesmerizing. You will them break through the bottom of the cloud, revealing the extensiveness of the cave. When ascending you will also see some artifacts that were discovered in the cavern from sacrificial offerings conducted by the Mayans. This whole experience could only be described as a spiritual one.
